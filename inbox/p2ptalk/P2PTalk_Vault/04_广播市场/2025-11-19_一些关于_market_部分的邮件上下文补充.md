### 1. chenmoonmo <chenmoonmo@gmail.com>  ·  2025-11-19 23:45

关于 market 部分，中本聪与之相关的邮件

<aside> 👉

2009/04/14 https://plan99.net/~mike/satoshi-emails/thread1.html

I started implementing a marketplace feature earlier that facilitates 
offering things for sale and taking orders, it's only half done though.  A 
bit like e-bay but without auctions, just "buy now".  Among other things, 
it would make it easy for anyone to offer currency exchange.

我之前开始开发一个市场功能，方便用户发布商品和接收订单，不过目前只完成了一半。有点像 
eBay，但没有拍卖，只有“立即购买”。除此之外，它还能让任何人轻松提供货币兑换服务。

</aside>

<aside> 👉

2010/03/03 https://satoshi.nakamotoinstitute.org/posts/bitcointalk/88/

When there's enough scale, maybe there can be an exchange site that doesn't 
do transfers, just matches up buyers and sellers to exchange with each 
other directly, similar to how e-bay works.

This would be a step better than e-bay.  E-bay manages to work fine even 
though shipped goods can't be recovered if payment falls through.

To make it safer, the exchange site could act as an escrow for the bitcoin 
side of the payment.  The seller puts the bitcoin payment in escrow, and 
the buyer sends the conventional payment directly to the seller.  The 
exchange service doesn't handle any real world money.

当规模足够大时，或许可以建立一个不进行转账的交易网站，只撮合买家和卖家直接进行交易，类似于 eBay 的运作方式。

为了提高安全性，交易平台可以作为比特币支付的托管方。卖家将比特币支付存入托管账户，买家则直接向卖家支付传统货币。该交易平台不经手任何真实货币。

这比 eBay 要好。eBay 虽然在付款失败后无法追回已发货的商品，但仍然能够正常运作。

</aside>

<aside> 👉

2011/03/09 https://www.bitcoin.com/satoshi-archive/emails/mike-hearn/14/

There's an unfinished part of the protocol that deals with setting up 
publisher/subscriber channels for distributed routing via the network. What 
was the purpose of this? Was the idea to have a p2p market or did it have 
some kind of lower level function, like perhaps broadcasting expected tx 
fees?

I was trying to implement an eBay style marketplace built in to the client. 
Publish/subscribe would be used for broadcasting product offers and 
ratings/reviews. Your reviews would be weighted by the blocks you've 
generated. I rightly abandoned it in favour of JSON-RPC, so other authors 
could implement it externally. The publish/subscribe "meet in the middle" 
mechanism was an interesting concept, but nothing remains that uses it.

It was part of writing code to explore the most technically demanding use 
cases and make sure Bitcoin could support everything that might be needed 
in the future, given the locked-in nature of the rules once the block chain 
started.

我试图在客户端中实现一个eBay风格的市集。发布/订阅将用于广播产品供应和评分/评论。你的评论将根据你生成的区块进行加权。我正确地放弃了它，转而使用JSON-RPC，这样其他作者可以外部实现它。发布/订阅的“中间会面”机制是一个有趣的概念，但没有任何东西使用它。

这是编写代码的一部分，用于探索最技术要求高的用例，并确保比特币能够支持未来可能需要的所有东西，考虑到区块链规则一旦开始锁定，就无法更改。

</aside>

中本聪在一些私人邮件中提到了这部分代码，但从我的解读来看，比特币——一种内在通信信道中的传递的货币诞生了，但是我们需要去哪里使用呢。中本聪一开始似乎是想提供一个 
e-bay 类似的市场，09 年说这部分代码只完成了一半（而不是我们理解的完全写完了），甚至直到 2010 年他还是这么想的，但是从 08 
年之后他就没有继续这部分，而是转向于 JSON-RPC。

而一开始提供这个类似 e-bay 
的市场的目的也很明确，就是为比特币提供初始价值，并回答我们一开始的问题，去哪里使用。在下面这个帖子里，中本聪设想了一个“可以在通信信道传输的稀缺的灰色贱金属”

<aside> 👉

https://bitcointalk.org/index.php?topic=583.5;wap2 

If it somehow acquired any value at all for whatever reason, then anyone 
wanting to transfer wealth over a long distance could buy some, transmit 
it, and have the recipient sell it. 

如果它以某种原因获得任何价值，那么任何想要长距离转移财富的人都可以购买一些，传输它，并让接收者卖掉它。

</aside>

随着越来越多的人使用它进行传输，其流动性增加，价格波动减小，进而吸引更多人将其作为价值存储或交换媒介，最终演变为货币。

而同样的描述在 2009/05/03 给 Sirius 整理的常见问答中也有出现:

<aside> 👉

2010/07/27 https://mmalmi.github.io/satoshi/ 

Historically, people have taken up scarce commodities as money, if 
necessary taking up whatever is at hand, such as shells or stones. Each has 
a kernel of usefulness that helped bootstrap the process, but the monetary 
value ends up being much more than the functional value alone. Most of the 
value comes from the value that others place in it.

看懂了，重点不是这个市场多牛逼，重点是通过他设计这个市场来看他比特币的设计思路。
通过区块进行加权有点意思。
目前我理解防女巫的方法都是用cpupower找nonce，但面对不同的一致性需求时怎么处理nonce是另外一回事。比特币是通过难度调整算法在处理nonce的，因为这里要求全局一致，但声誉不同，所以这里需要一个别的什么算法来满足这个声誉的一致性的问题。
